Mine location(image)
Mponeng, located near Carletonville, South Africa, is the world's deepest gold mine, Commissioned in 1986 by AngloGold Ashanti, the mine was formerly known as Western Deep Levels South Shaft. Harmony Gold Mining Company Limited acquired it in 2020. Development: Surface sinking for the twin shaft system began in 1981. Production Start: Mining commenced in 1986, along with the commissioning of the gold plant. Naming: It was originally part of the Western Deep Levels complex before being renamed Mponeng in 1999. Ownership Transition: Harmony Gold acquired the mine from AngloGold Ashanti in October 2020 for $300 million. Operations & Depth: The mine targets the Ventersdorp Contact Reef (VCR), It is renowned as one of the world's most significant deep-level mining operations, often relying on massive refrigeration systems to maintain bearable working conditions.
